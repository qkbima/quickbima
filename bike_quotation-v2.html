Contact the insurer -

Notify HDFC Ergo's call centre about the accident at 1800-2700-700.

Provide the agent with the policy number, accident location, time, damage, etc.

Agent will give you a claim number and direct you to the nearest garage/workshop.

TIP: Don't leave the vehicle unattended or unlocked, as any theft of car parts or accessories thereafter will not be honoured by the insurer.

2

Get Your Car Inspected -

A surveyor shall inspect your car within 2 working days and give on-the-spot approval.

Documents that you need to fill up or submit at this stage include

1.) Claim form

2.) Copy of RC

3.) Copy of driver's license etc.

TIP: Never start repairs on the car without any survey or inspection by the insurer's representative.

3

Get Back your spruced-up Car -

The car shall be released to you post-repair only after your confirmation that the same has been repaired as per your satisfaction.

HDFC Ergo shall settle the claim directly with the workshop while you will have to pay any balance that wasn't claimable under the policy terms.

Follow these steps when claiming reimbursement from your insurer-

1.) Pay for the repairs and collect all invoices & payment receipts from the workshop

2.) Approach your nearest insurer branch or courier the documents

3.) Be patient. Insurer takes 7-15 days to reimburse, after receipt of completed documents & bills

1

Contact the insurer -

Notify HDFC Ergo's call centre about the theft at 1800-2700-700.

Provide the agent with the policy number, theft location, time, etc.

The agent shall direct you to file an FIR with the police immediately.

After filing an FIR, you need to get back to the agent for filing the claim form & related documents

2

Assist with Investigation -

HDFC Ergo may employ specialist investigators to try to trace your car. The specialist often helps you get all the necessary documents from the police.

Usually, the following documents are sought in case of a theft claim -

1.) Claim form

2.) Original Registration Certificate (RC)

3.) Copy of driver's license

4.) Police FIR

5.) Entire set of vehicle keys

6.) Intimation to the RTO that the car has been stolen

7.) Indemnity Bond

8.) Subrogation Letter

9.) No Objection Certificate (NOC) from financier

10.) No Trace Report from the police (A 'No Trace Report' is issued by the police if they fail to trace the vehicle within 90 days from the date of theft).

3

Receive Compensation -

The settlement amount is based on the Insured Declared Value (IDV) of your car. The final settled amount might, however, differ based on usage and market value.

If there is an outstanding loan towards the purchase of your car, then HDFC Ergo will settle the amount directly with the financier.

Inform the insurer in the event the police recovers your car. HDFC Ergo is liable to pay compensation on damages caused to the vehicle and for stolen items as per the terms of your policy.
