Meaning of Travel Insurance

Travel insurance is a type of insurance policy that helps covering the policyholder against incidents like flight cancellation or delay, losing of checked baggage, trip cancellation, emergency situations, and loss of passport. Buying travel insurance is recommended at the time of booking a trip to allow coverage for the entire trip which can either be a multi-trip or single-trip policy. The travel insurance plans cover medical expenses that are exceptionally high in some foreign countries.

Important Aspects of a Good Travel Insurance

Getting an online travel insurance plan will ensure that you have unforeseen situations and emergencies covered for you while you are travelling. Some of the key aspects of a plan that you must look out for are:

  • Compensate your Flight Issues: Your plan must provide compensation in cases that include flight cancellation and delay. This is usually availed when you are travelling abroad. It also provides accommodation throughout the waiting phase.
  • Hospitalization and Medical Emergencies: In the case, you go through any serious medical emergency during your travel; a good travel policy will bear all the expenses that might include medical treatment or hospitalization.
  • Accommodation and Travel: Any emergency leading to trip delay or termination is covered by the travel insurance. Expenses that may arise from in-between transportation, air ambulance (in case of medical emergency) or hotel accommodation are also taken care of by the travel insurance plan.
  • Legal Expenses and Liability: Apart from the accommodation and medical costs, the insurance plan also provides coverage for legal expenses and legal liabilities whatsoever.

What to Consider before selecting a Travel Insurance Plan

  • Affordability: Costs will have their own significance when you select travel insurance plan. It is better to go with minimum premiums that are convenient and easily fit your budget to make your insurance cheap and affordable.
  • Credibility of the Company: It is very important to build trust with the company who is going to be your insurance provider. You will have the faith that the company will deliver all the promises in time.
  • Destination: The premium required to be paid will depend on your destination greatly. The premium coverage will be higher in places where health care services are expensive.
  • Hassle-free: Convenience should be your first priority while selecting an insurance plan.
  • Purpose of the Trip: If you trip involves a lot of adventure sports, it is better to opt for a special plan that provides adequate coverage. In the case, your trip is a more relaxing one; you can go with the basic insurance plan.
  • Duration of the Trip: If your trip is of a longer duration, your premium will be higher, and vice versa. For frequent fryers, choosing an annual multi-trip plan will be an economic option.
  • Sum Insured: The premium will greatly depend on the sum insured. The policy cost will be higher if the insured sum is high.
  • Age: Premiums differ depending on the age of the person. The plan for a 45 years old person would vary from a 25 years old individual.
  • Coverage: Having add-ons in your plan will have an effect on your premium. Add-ons help you widen the bracket of the coverage plan.
  • Claim Settlement Percentage: The number of claims the insurance company has settles gives the company an edge as compared to other companies.
  • Solvency Ratio: The solvency ratio is important for understanding the goodwill and steadiness of the insurance company. It shows the ability of the company to deliver its promises without being bankrupt.

Type of travel insurance plans

Depending on your requirement, you can choose whichever coverage you want.

Domestic travel insurance

This insurance coverage is for people travelling inside the country. It includes coverage for permanent death or disability, medical emergencies, travel delay, personal liability, and stolen or lost baggage.

International travel insurance

It provides coverage for contingencies that travellers might experience during overseas trip. It includes hijack, overseas medical expenses, lost baggage, travel delays, loss of documents or passport, evacuation/repatriations to India, etc.

Corporate travel insurance

Under this plan, employees working in an organisation will get coverage for domestic as well as international trips.

Medical travel insurance

This is a short-term insurance plan that covers medical and health care expenses which may occur in evacuation or medical emergencies while travelling overseas. The exact exclusions and inclusions will depend mainly on the travel insurance providers.

Student travel insurance

This policy is mainly for students who are travelling overseas for pursuing their higher studies. It will include medical treatment, study interruptions, passport loss, etc. The paperwork is quite less for student plans.

Family travel insurance

This plan would include the insurance of the entire family for travel emergencies which may include baggage loss, hospitalization, or other expenses. The disbursement of the claim is easy with minimum amount of paperwork.

Senior citizen travel insurance

This plan is for people with ages ranging from 61-70 years. It is to make sure that the travel of senior citizens is safe and comfortable. It will include coverage for cashless hospitalization, dental treatments, and other related usual benefits.

Individual travel insurance

It is meant for only one individual to cover the travel related contingencies. It will provide coverage for home burglary, trip curtailment, and trip cancellation.

Single trip insurance

It provides coverage only fir a single trip. It takes care of non-medical as well as medical emergencies.

Multi-trip travel insurance

It provides coverage for a span of one year, specially designed for people who travel frequently so that they will not have to apply for insurance every time.

Benefits of Having Travel Insurance

Frequent travels get to deal with stolen bags, cancelled flights, trip delay, or even lost passports. Such mishaps can easily derail a perfectly planned out trip. Having travel insurance will let to effectively deal with such situations without ruining the entire travel journey.

Exclusions

Some of the common exclusions in travel insurance plans include:

  • Loss/damage of keys.
  • Trains/flights missed because of civil war or protests.
  • A baggage delay of than 24 hours.
  • Pre-existing conditions that may lead to hospitalization.

You should note that the exclusions will differ depending on the plan you choose. Therefore, it is important that you compare the quotes of insurance plans online.
