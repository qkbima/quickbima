How to Choose the Right Type of Life Insurance Plan

Having a life insurance cover is essential in this day and age, given that the cost of living just keeps rising. While having adequate insurance coverage is not going to bring back your loved one, it can certainly provide the financial security you need for survival. Several people have said that they were able to live comfortably after the demise of their spouse because of the insurance policy availed by their partner. Now, deciding which insurance plan to buy is not an easy task. Numerous players have entered the market, offering customers a variety of choices that come with different benefits and features. If you have decided to buy a life insurance policy, you need to determine if you want a term life cover or a whole life cover.

Life Insurance can be Divided into Two Main Categories:

Term Life Insurance and Whole Life Insurance. Term insurance is a kind of insurance policy that provides coverage for a specific period of time, such as 10, 20 or 30 years. Once the policy expires, it is up to the policyholder whether he or she wants to renew it or let it end. On the other hand, whole life cover is in force for the entire lifetime of the insured, provided that premiums are regularly paid. The policy covers the insured till the time of his or her death or when the insured reaches 100 years of age, whichever comes first.

Why Consider Term Insurance

Term insurance is cheaper compared to a whole life cover. Recently, several insurers have begun offering survival benefits along with term plans. It has been observed that people opt for this kind of cover to help their dependents during times when they have significant financial obligation, such as mortgage. People have also been increasingly turning to term life policies to help pay for their children’s education.

Why Consider Whole Life Insurance

The advantage of whole life insurance is that your dependants are guaranteed to receive the death benefit regardless of when you die. Cash value is one of the key selling points for life insurers. Policyholders are eligible to cash value, which is an account within your insurance policy that grows slowly and is tax-deferred. As the cash value builds, policyholders can avail loans against the accumulated funds.

Other Considerations

After you have decided between a term and a whole life cover, request and compare quotes of the different insurance companies. The easiest way to compare quotes is through insurance web aggregator QuickBima, where you can avail information in the quickest time.
