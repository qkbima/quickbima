Life insurance is a form of contractual binding agreed upon by the insurer and the insurance service provider. Under this agreement, the service provider agrees to pay a fixed sum to the insurer if there is an untimely event such as death. This event may, if agreed upon by both parties, be the incidence of a disease or critical illness. The insurer, for his or her part, will pay a fixed sum of money over a stipulated period to ensure that he or she makes the agreed amount as payment towards the insurance. In some cases, the funeral expenses may also be included under the sum paid as benefits.

Types of life insurance policies

When choosing a life insurance policy, you must see what kind of policy the company has. All the life insurance types fall into five categories. It will be one of these:

a) Term Insurance
b) ULIP (Unit Linked Policy Plan)
c) Whole Life Insurance
d) Endowment Policy
e) Money Back Life Insurance

Depending on what you hope to achieve, you can choose one insurance policy. Here is the detail of each of the five types of life insurance policy you can choose from.

Term Insurance

Among all types of life insurances, this is the simplest. It gives you a financial protection for a fixed term such as 15 or 20 years. Under this policy, your family will get a fixed amount of money in case you die so that the family is safe financially. But, in case you survive, the insurance service provider needs to pay nothing. The plus point of the term insurance is that it has a low premium for the service it provides. Many people consider this as one of the best life insurance plans in India.

More complex insurance plans

ULIP (Unit Linked Policy Plan)

In this plan, a part of the premium is used for providing the life insurance cover. The rest of the amount goes to equities and debts investment. The investment is highly risky and may not provide capital gains. One must have a regular habit of investing if one wants to use this plan.


Whole Life Insurance

This has the double benefit of investment and insurance. The whole life insurance plans assure the person is insured for his whole life or 100 years whichever is earlier. The insurer calculates interest on the sum assured which is the amount that the insurer pays to the nominee in the case of the death of the policyholder.

Endowment Policy

Under this plan too, you get the double benefit of life insurance and investment. A small part of the premium goes to the sum assured. The rest of the money is invested in the asset market involving equities and debt. The policyholder gets the lump sum amount after the expiry of the stipulated time, or the nominee gets the amount upon death, whichever occurs first. There are also cases where endowment plans give bonuses regularly or after the death or maturity of the policy.

Money Back Life Insurance

When the policy holder is alive, he or she will get partial survival benefits in the form of periodical payments. If the policyholder dies, the insurer pays the full sum assured along with survival benefits.

Things to consider when you buy Life insurance

Lower Premium: You can avail of the offer of lower premium if you are young. The younger you are, the lower the premium is. So, those who join early will get the most benefit.

Go online: This is an effortless way to manage your insurance policy account. The interaction is directly with the service provider, and so you do not pay any extra, unnecessary money. Policies are more accessible you get many time-saving features.

See into the future: What seems huge now will not be so after 20 years. So, you must take inflation into the calculation and then plan your future.

Calculate your needs exactly: When you calculate the coverage you need, check what your future plans are and then take a policy that covers everything. If you need a protection plan, then opt for the life insurance term plans.

Things that determine your Life Insurance premium

Your current age: This is the most important thing since you pay a lower premium if your age is lower. This is because the risk for the insurer increases if the age increases. So, make it a point to apply fast.

Coverage tenure: The short-term coverage is less risky for the insurer than a long-term one. This is why the long-term coverage costs less.

Smoker or not: This is another important aspect of the coverage. Smokers have more risk, and so the insurer will decide based on that.

Gender: Women live longer, and so they have a slightly lower premium.
